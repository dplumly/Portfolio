@ZOMBIESELFIE

To spice up the office around Halloween I decided to make a molded zombie arm that takes pictures of zombie lunch and posted them to the Twitter account @ZombieSelfie.

The build used a molded silicone arm (happens to be mine and my hair is still growing back), a custom steel mount to clip to an office partition, and a vibration sensor hooked up to an Arduino. Once the arm is slapped a photo will be taken using an IR Led and passed to the Eye-Fi card in the camera. The photo is then put into a Dropbox folder and If This Then That (IFTTT) posts the photo to Twitter.

SKILLS USED:

Silicone Moulding, Painting, Arduino

MOONSHOT BUILD

At Team One we have a series called Moonshot where people come in and tell us about the work they are a part of. At this one was Brent Bushnell from Two Bit Circus. Two Bit Circus does really cool builds to help kids learn core principles in an interactive way.

For this event the creative technologist group was asked to build an installation with the concept “Science is then new rock and roll.” With that in mind we built a carnival like installation “Brain vs Brawn.” We made 7ft guitars with a ladder of LEDs up the neck. The bass guitar was the brawn in which we made a high-striker carnival game. The second guitar was a hacked EEG that gave readings on how hard you could concentrate.

SKILLS USED:

3D Modeling/Printing, Woodworking, Arduino

DUSTIN'S WORDS

Imagine a life where you could understand everything that is being said, but physically can’t communicate back. That is a common occurrence for people who suffer from Autism. The project started with Matt Reamer making a device for his brother Dustin to be able to communicate with his Mom over text messages. We started with Dustin, but are now looking to help others. We are finding issues with the cost and functionality of similar devices on the market. Our goal is to come up with solutions for anyone that suffers from communication complications and make devices obtainable. For more info go to DUSTINSWORDS.

SKILLS USED:

3D Modeling/Printing, Particle.io, Web Dev, Photography

PAPERPROTO

Matt Reamer and I are always looking for ways to make prototyping better for users. In this search we found a prototyping tool called the Iron phone. It was a great tool, but the people that made it didn’t release it to the public. Obviously, we saw this opportunity to help the community so we created to create an open source prototyping tool called PAPERPROTO.

SKILLS USED:

3D Printing/Modeling, Web Design, Art Direction

LEXUS FUTURE CONCEPT VEHICLE

The Future Concept Vehicle site was created to bring more life and differentiate future concept vehicles from current vehicles and other competitors sites. We introduced a ton of new interactions, layouts, image galleries and more that make it different from Lexus.com, while still keeping core components to make it feel unified. My role was to prototype concepts to show interactions to the team and the clients giving a visual representation of the way the site could look and feel.

SKILLS USED:

Concepting, Prototyping, Interaction and Web Design

CT PERSPECTIVE

CT Perspective was born from a hackathon for one of our creative technologist classes while in grad school. We wanted to show that Creative Technologists work should be looked at through a different lens. The way we accomplished this was by taking off the polarizing filters on computer monitors. We took the lenses out of 3D glasses and put them into metal gears. Since metal is conductive one of the gears would tweet when someone touched it to see what was on the screens.

SKILLS USED:

Carpentry, Arduino, Raspberry Pi, and some Magic!

ANGER BANGER

Sitting at a desk all day can be a very traumatic experience making one angry by the end of the day. As a way to relieve this anger we created the Anger Banger. The Anger Banger is a carnival style punching machine used from an old punching bag lying around our school. We used an Arduino as the guts along with a pressure sensor, that when hit at a certain force would light up one to five LEDs explaining how manly you were or weren’t.

SKILLS USED:

Carpentry, Arduino, Videography, Art Direction
